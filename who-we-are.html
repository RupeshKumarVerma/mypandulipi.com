Our Story

Who We Are

mypandulipi isn't run by a company. It's run by a husband and wife from Indore who believe scripture deserves to be held, not just read. This is why we started, and what we're trying to do.

CS
Chandni Soni
Prabhusevika

Chandni studied engineering and worked for years at Tesco in Bengaluru. After she got married, she moved to Indore. Today, she runs the home and takes care of the family, and she runs mypandulipi with the same care and discipline, every single day.

mypandulipi was born from her heart. She has always felt close to the Ramayana and to Lord Shiva, and she prays every day. One question kept coming back to her: why do we read scripture from a plain printed book now, when it used to be handwritten and bound with so much love? That one question became mypandulipi.

Chandni looks after everything. She chooses the materials, checks the quality, and packs every single order herself, all while running her home. Her dream is simple but big: to see one of these books in every home that wants one. She believes this brings families closer to Sanatan tradition, and helps them truly feel God's presence, not just read about it.

RKV
Rupesh Kumar Verma
Prabhusevak

Rupesh works as an engineer in an IT company in Indore. But outside his job, many people know him for something else: his beautiful bhajan singing, and the way he leads Sundarkand path in homes and offices almost every Saturday.

He grew up near Braj in Uttar Pradesh, the land of Krishna and Radha. His love for them started when he was just a child, long before mypandulipi was even an idea. For years, he led Sundarkand path purely as seva. Meeting hundreds of devotees every year, he saw how much people still long to hold scripture in their hands, not just see it on a screen.

Rupesh started mypandulipi together with his wife, Chandni. They both want the same simple thing: to put these sacred books in every home that wants one, so people feel more positive, more strong, and more connected to God in their everyday life.

How it started

Two paths to the same idea

Chandni's devotion to the Ramayana and to Shiva, and Rupesh's lifelong closeness to Krishna and Radha and his years on the Sundarkand path circuit, came from very different places: an engineer-turned-homemaker in Indore, and a path pravachak native to Braj. But both kept arriving at the same realization: scripture that's merely printed gets read once and shelved, while scripture that's handbound gets kept, touched, and returned to.

That shared realization is what turned into mypandulipi, a small, family-run effort to bring manuscript-style Sundarkand and Hanuman Chalisa into homes across India, one order packed by the two of them at a time.
