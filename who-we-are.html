Our Story

Who We Are

mypandulipi isn't run by a company — it's run by a husband and wife from Indore who believe scripture deserves to be held, not just read. This is why we started, and what we're trying to do.

CS
Chandni Soni
Founder

Chandni is an engineer by training, and spent years in operations with Tesco in Bengaluru before her marriage brought her to Indore. Today she manages her home and family full-time — and it is that same quiet discipline that runs mypandulipi from the ground up.

mypandulipi exists because of her devotion first. A lifelong closeness to the Ramayana, to Lord Shiva, and to her daily spiritual practice kept pulling at one question: why does scripture in most homes sit as a printed paperback, when it once lived as something handwritten, bound and kept for generations? That question became mypandulipi.

Chandni manages mypandulipi end to end — sourcing, quality, packaging, every order — alongside running her home. Her goal is simple to say and enormous in scope: get a pandulipi into every home that wants one, so that Sanatan practice grows stronger one household at a time, and people feel — not just read about — the power of the almighty.

RKV
Rupesh Kumar Verma
Co-founder

Rupesh is an engineer working in an IT company in Indore by profession — and, outside of that, one of the city's known voices for bhajan singing and Sundarkand path orations, leading gatherings in homes and offices most Saturdays.

He grew up near Braj, Uttar Pradesh — the land of Krishna and Radha — and that closeness to the divine has stayed with him since childhood, long before mypandulipi existed. Years of leading Sundarkand path for free, purely as seva, put him in front of hundreds of devotees a year and showed him first-hand how much people still crave a real, tactile connection to scripture.

Rupesh co-founded mypandulipi.com alongside his wife Chandni with one shared objective: to put manuscript-style granths into every home willing to have one, so the sacred strength held in these pages can reach people as positivity, resolve and quiet strength in daily life.

How it started

Two paths to the same idea

Chandni's devotion to the Ramayana and to Shiva, and Rupesh's lifelong closeness to Krishna and Radha and his years on the Sundarkand path circuit, came from very different places — an engineer-turned-homemaker in Indore, and a path pravachak native to Braj. But both kept arriving at the same realization: scripture that's merely printed gets read once and shelved; scripture that's handbound gets kept, touched, and returned to.

That shared realization is what turned into mypandulipi — a small, family-run effort to bring manuscript-style Sundarkand and Hanuman Chalisa into homes across India, one order packed by the two of them at a time.
